# Nutri Snap — App Description (user-value perspective)

> **Status:** reflects v1 scope + three features promoted to "main" on 2026-06-26

> **Last updated:** 2026-06-26

---

**Nutri Snap** is a gentle nutrition companion for people who want to eat well — and age well — without the guilt.

You take a photo of your meal. That's it. No searching databases, no weighing portions, no logging every gram. The app sees what's on your plate and quietly records it — calories, protein, and the nutrients that actually matter — as a warm little summary you can glance at and move on with your day.

What makes it different is the spirit behind it. Most food apps are scolds: they tally your "budget," flash red when you go over, and turn eating into anxious accounting. Nutri Snap is the opposite. There's no failing here. A fuller day shows up in soft amber, never alarm-red. The whole experience is built to make you feel *aware*, not *judged* — a calm coach, not a calorie cop.

It also cares about far more than calories. Beyond the usual numbers, it pays attention to the quiet things that shape how you look and feel over the years — fiber, omega-3, vitamin C, iron, zinc, magnesium and more. And it never grades you day by day. One indulgent dinner doesn't ruin anything. It watches your *patterns* over time, the way a thoughtful friend would: "you've been a little low on omega-3 this week," not "you failed today."

Every meal also comes with a gentle read on how it's likely to make you *feel* — a simple, wordless sense of whether it's slow, steady energy or a quick spike-and-dip. No glucose monitor, no numbers, no lectures about "bad carbs" — just a soft cue that helps you understand your own energy, your focus, and the steadiness that matters for long-term health and clearer skin.

There are three simple places to be:

- **Snap** — point, shoot, done. Your meal logs itself, energy read and all.
- **Journal** — a soft, color-coded calendar of your days. Tap any one to revisit what you ate, like a little photo diary of your meals.
- **Trends** — where the real value lives: calm insight into your eating rhythms and whether you're truly getting enough of what your body needs, plus an optional weekly reflection that reads like a kind note, not a report card.

You don't even have to open the app to stay aware. A quiet **home-screen widget** shows where your day stands at a glance — ambient encouragement, never a nag tugging for your attention.

And your nutrition doesn't live in a silo. It connects to **Apple Health**, so your meals become part of the bigger picture of your wellbeing — flowing alongside your weight, your activity, your movement — and your targets quietly stay current as your body changes, with nothing extra to enter.

It's built for the long game: for the person who wants to take better care of themselves for the sake of energy, health, and aging gracefully — not to crash-diet or obsess. It's quietly personalized to you, respects your time (logging takes seconds), respects your privacy (your meal photos stay on your phone), and respects your peace of mind (no account to create, no guilt-tripping).

In one line: **it's the food app for people who hate food apps** — effortless to use, impossible to fail at, and genuinely on your side.
